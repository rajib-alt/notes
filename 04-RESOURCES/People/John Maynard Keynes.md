## ✍️ Style & Themes
 **Writing Style:** Analytical yet polemical, combines rigorous economic theory with passionate moral argument; clear, persuasive prose with striking imagery
  
 **Core Themes:** 
- Government intervention to address unemployment & recessions 
- Aggregate demand as driver of economic activity 
- Critique of laissez-faire & classical economics 
- Role of uncertainty & "animal spirits" in markets 
- Protectionism over free trade (post-1929) 
- Full employment & social justice

 **Influences:** Alfred Marshall, G.E. Moore, Thomas Malthus, Knut Wicksell, Jeremy Bentham, Edmund Burke, John Neville Keynes (father), Silvio Gesell

**Notes:** 
- Founded **Keynesian economics** — school of thought bearing his name 
- Considered **"father of macroeconomics"** & most influential economist of 20th century 
- Revolutionized economic thinking in 1930s, overturning neoclassical full-employment assumption 
- Advocated **fiscal & monetary policy** to mitigate recessions 
- Argued **aggregate demand** determines economic activity; inadequate demand → prolonged unemployment 
- Introduced **price stickiness** concept & **liquidity preference** theory 
- Led British delegation at 1944 **Bretton Woods Conference**; helped design IMF & World Bank 
- Created hereditary peerage: **1st Baron Keynes of Tilton** (1942) 
- Married Lydia Lopokova (ballerina) in 1925 
- 2008 financial crisis sparked **Keynesian resurgence** 
- Time magazine: "Most Important People of the Century" (1999)
